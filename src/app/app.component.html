Frequently Asked Questions

Clarity and Compliance: Everything You Need to Know About Healthy Homes & Meth Safety

What is a Healthy Homes Assessment in New Zealand?

A Healthy Homes Assessment is a property inspection that checks whether a rental home meets the five key standards set by the New Zealand Government: heating, insulation, ventilation, moisture ingress and drainage, and draught stopping.

Is a Healthy Homes Report mandatory for landlords in NZ?

Yes. As of July 2021, all private landlords in New Zealand must ensure their rental properties comply with Healthy Homes Standards within 90 days of any new or renewed tenancy.

What does a Healthy Homes inspection include?

Our certified inspectors check heating sources, ceiling and underfloor insulation, bathroom and kitchen ventilation, ground moisture barriers, drainage systems, and draughts around windows and doors.

How long does a Healthy Homes inspection take?

Most inspections take between 60–90 minutes, depending on the size and condition of the property. A detailed report is typically delivered within 24–48 hours.

What is Meth Testing in New Zealand?

Meth testing, also known as P testing, screens a property for contamination caused by methamphetamine use or manufacture. This is especially important before buying, renting, or moving into a home.

Why should I test my property for meth contamination?

Meth contamination can pose serious health risks. Testing ensures peace of mind for landlords, home buyers, and tenants, and may also be required by insurers or lenders.

How is meth testing carried out?

We perform lab-backed surface sampling following NZS 8510:2017 guidelines. All samples are sent to IANZ-accredited labs for analysis, with results usually available within 48 hours.

What is the safe meth contamination limit in NZ?

According to NZS 8510:2017, the recommended maximum acceptable level of methamphetamine contamination is 1.5 µg/100cm² for residential properties.
